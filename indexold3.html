Short Biography

I’m a 2nd Year PhD Student and a PTDF Scholar in the Cloud Modeling & Simulation Research Group at the University of Bradford, under the supervision of Professor Irfan Awan, Dr. Mariam Kiran and Professor Hassan Ugail. I obtained a Bachelor of Engineering (B. Eng.) in Electrical & Computer Engineering and a Masters of Science (MSc) in Control Systems Engineering from the Department of Automatic Control and Systems Engineering, University of Sheffield United Kingdom in 2012.

Research Interest

My current work is focused on design and development of integrated optimized Fault tolerance models and algorithms for a typical cloud environment. I’m also developing some predictive fault tolerance models by applying machine learning algorithm on real time cloud failure data.

I have been exploring various platforms such as Openstack, AWS, Microsoft Azure and high performance computing grids. I am one of the designers and developers of a private cloud platform at the University of Bradford called Bradstack, which is currently working on infrastructure related issues particularly with AWS and Openstack.

My current research interest include:

  • Fault Tolerance in Cloud

  • Distributed and parallel computing

  • Performance related issues on Cloud Platforms

  • Cloud Platform implementation (Openstack, AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud Platform)

  • Machine Learning

  • Automatic Control systems
